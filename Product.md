# Golden Italy — Product Plan & Brand Identity

Status: Phase 0 / planning  
Last updated: 13 July 2026  
Primary market: Lido di Ostia, Rome, Italy  
Primary language: Italian; English may be added as a secondary language after launch scope is confirmed.

## 1. Product summary

Golden Italy needs a cinematic, mobile-first restaurant landing page that turns local discovery traffic into table reservations, phone calls, and directions. The experience is a guided visual tour rather than a conventional restaurant website: visitors arrive from the street, cross the illuminated threshold, move through the dining room, discover table details and atmosphere, encounter a small editorial selection of dishes, and finish at the reservation invitation.

This is not positioned as a formal luxury restaurant or a generic tourist venue. The website should communicate an accessible, generous, recognisably Italian and Roman dining experience with pizza, seafood, Mediterranean dishes, and late-evening energy.

### Primary objective

Make “Prenota un tavolo” the clearest action on every viewport.

### Secondary objectives

- Let visitors call the restaurant in one tap.
- Make address, directions, hours, cuisine, and the character of the restaurant easy to understand.
- Present the atmosphere and food without forcing visitors through long copy.
- Let visitors feel the progression from the Ostia street to the dining table.
- Build trust with accurate practical information and owned imagery.
- Support search visibility for restaurant, pizzeria, seafood, and Lido di Ostia intent.

### Core conversion events

1. Reservation CTA click.
2. Phone link click.
3. Directions/map click.
4. Tour progression to the dining-room and dish chapters.
5. Final reservation CTA exposure and click.

## 3. Audience

### Primary: local intent

Residents and returning guests in Ostia looking for an easy lunch, dinner, pizza, or seafood option. They value speed, practical information, familiar flavours, and a clear way to reserve or call.

### Secondary: Rome coast visitors

Italian and international visitors staying in Ostia or travelling between the coast, Ostia Antica, Fiumicino, and Rome. They need immediate clarity on location, cuisine, accessibility, price expectations, and language.

### Occasion segments

- Couples seeking a relaxed evening meal.
- Families and small groups.
- Friends meeting for pizza, pasta, or seafood.
- Visitors searching on mobile shortly before eating.

## 4. Positioning

### Positioning statement

For locals and visitors who want an unpretentious Italian table in Lido di Ostia, Golden Italy is a lively restaurant and pizzeria that brings Roman comfort, seafood, pizza, and a warm nighttime atmosphere together in one accessible place.

### Brand promise

“Una tavola accesa nel cuore di Ostia.”

This line is a proposed creative direction, not an existing business slogan.

### Value pillars

1. **Warm arrival** — the illuminated storefront and open interior create an immediate sense of welcome.
2. **Familiar abundance** — pizza, pasta, Roman cooking, and seafood offer broad group appeal.
3. **Ostia character** — the site belongs to the Roman coast, not to a generic “Italian restaurant” template.
4. **Simple access** — reserve, call, find the restaurant, and preview its dishes without friction.

## 5. Brand identity

### Brand essence

**Ostia after dark: warm, lively, generous.**

### Personality

- Warm, never precious.
- Confident, never loud.
- Cinematic, never over-produced.
- Local, never folkloric.
- Direct, never corporate.

### Voice

Use short, sensory Italian with practical clarity. Prefer concrete invitations over superlatives.

**Do:** “Pizza, cucina romana e sapori di mare nel cuore di Ostia.”  
**Do:** “Scegli il tavolo. Al resto pensiamo noi.”  
**Avoid:** “La migliore esperienza gastronomica d’Italia.”  
**Avoid:** unverified claims such as “family-run,” “fresh every day,” “award-winning,” or “number one.”

### Hero copy direction

**Eyebrow:** Ristorante · Pizzeria · Lido di Ostia  
**Headline:** Una tavola accesa nel cuore di Ostia.  
**Supporting line:** Pizza, cucina romana e sapori di mare, dal pranzo alla sera.  
**Primary CTA:** Prenota un tavolo  
**Secondary CTA:** Chiama ora  
**Tertiary text link:** Entra nel ristorante

Final copy should be adjusted after reservation mechanics and hours are confirmed.

## 6. Visual identity system

The supplied video is the visual source of truth. It shows the Golden Italy exterior at night, warm interior lighting, pedestrians passing the storefront, reflective paving, and the red/green neon wordmark.

### Palette

| Token | Hex | Intended use |
|---|---:|---|
| Night Ink | `#0B0A08` | Primary background and video fallback |
| Warm Ivory | `#F7F0E3` | Primary text on dark surfaces |
| Espresso | `#2B1A12` | Warm secondary surfaces |
| Golden Amber | `#D49A50` | Primary CTA and focus accents |
| Neon Rosso | `#E33B32` | Brand detail, hover accent, sign reference |
| Neon Verde | `#2BC56C` | Brand detail, sign reference |
| Glass White | `rgba(255,255,255,0.10)` | Navigation and utility surfaces |

Use Warm Ivory for reading text. Red and green are identity accents and must never be the only way to communicate status or meaning. Every text/background pair must be checked to WCAG AA: 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.

### Typography

- **Display:** Instrument Serif, regular/italic. It connects to the selected MotionSites visual language and gives the hero warmth without imitating a traditional trattoria sign.
- **Body and UI:** Barlow, weights 400–600. It remains clear on video, maps, captions, buttons, and small mobile screens.
- **Fallbacks:** Georgia for display; Arial/Helvetica/sans-serif for body.

Use a restrained type scale. Body copy is at least 16px on mobile with a 1.5–1.7 line height. Avoid all-caps paragraphs; reserve uppercase for short labels.

### Logo direction

The neon “GOLDEN ITALY” sign is an authentic environmental brand asset and should remain visible in the hero footage. The digital header should use a clean text wordmark until an official vector logo is supplied. Do not trace, redraw, distort, or recolour the sign as a substitute for an approved logo.

### Photography

- Prioritise real food, interior, staff, and storefront photography owned or licensed by the restaurant.
- Warm highlights, deep shadows, true food colour, and natural table moments.
- Avoid stock images, excessive orange grading, fake steam, and AI-generated food images that could misrepresent the offer.
- Do not reuse Tripadvisor user photography without explicit rights.

### Shape and surface

- Spacious single-column composition with full-bleed media.
- Rounded pill CTAs and navigation, but not every card.
- Glass effects are limited to UI placed over video; use solid surfaces below the fold for readability.
- One clear elevation scale and a visible 2–3px keyboard focus ring.

### Pinterest UI/UX research direction

Pinterest research was used as a pattern library, not as a source to copy. The strongest references share four traits that fit Golden Italy:

- **Cinematic dark hospitality hero:** a warm interior or food image carries the first screen while a single reservation CTA remains unmistakable.
- **Minimal editorial navigation:** a small number of plain-language anchors, generous space, and large serif headlines let photography lead.
- **Alternating visual chapters:** full-bleed room views alternate with closer table/food details, creating the feeling of physically moving deeper into the restaurant.
- **Restrained dish gallery:** food appears as an emotional proof of the experience, not as a catalogue of items, categories, prices, or ordering controls.

Selected references:

- [Pinterest — modern restaurant template](https://it.pinterest.com/pin/438045501279208929/): useful for its dark hospitality setting, visible booking priority, and food photography paired with short text.
- [Pinterest — Laurent elegant restaurant theme](https://ru.pinterest.com/pin/laurent-elegant-restaurant-theme--921760248721123109/): useful for sophisticated minimalism, high-quality visual emphasis, and simple navigation.
- [Pinterest — clean and minimalist restaurant UI](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/clean-and-minimalist-restaurant-web-design--650840583676249166/): useful for reducing interface density around strong photography.
- [Pinterest — Italian pasta restaurant website](https://it.pinterest.com/pin/italian-pasta-restaurant-website-webmotion--364862007324938525/): useful as an Italian/editorial typography reference.
- [Pinterest — Mamma Mia Italian restaurant concept](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/mamma-mia-italian-restaurant-food-delivery-by-elizaveta-schukina--633387440688415/): useful for dark contrast, elegant typography, and an expressive circular CTA, while excluding its delivery/e-commerce patterns.

The resulting design is not fine-dining black-and-gold. Golden Italy retains the Pinterest references’ cinematic structure but replaces luxury cues with the real neon storefront, warmer convivial imagery, simpler language, and the more informal character of Ostia.

## 7. MotionSites prompt decision

### Selected free prompt: Visual Hero

Catalog ID: `visual-hero`  
Source: [MotionSites Visual Hero](https://motionsites.ai/?prompt=visual-hero)

This is the strongest fit because its structure is built around a fullscreen video, oversized editorial typography, a compact glass navigation pill, short supporting copy, direct CTA controls, and subtle pointer parallax. It lets the supplied storefront video carry the brand while keeping the booking action visible.

### Adaptation rules

- Replace the prompt’s original video with the supplied Golden Italy MP4.
- Replace the central demo/product language with restaurant positioning and reservation actions.
- Keep only one dominant CTA: “Prenota un tavolo.”
- Remove sign-in, pricing, template, and SaaS navigation concepts. Retain only a custom editorial gallery pattern that supports the restaurant tour.
- Do not use the prompt’s memory-heavy canvas frame-capture/boomerang technique. Use native video playback with a clean poster and an optional short crossfade at the loop point.
- Keep parallax desktop-only, under roughly 8px, and disable it for touch devices and `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Use a 55–65% black gradient/scrim behind critical text, adjusted per breakpoint.

### Rejected alternative: PROMPT

The free `PROMPT` landing page has an impressive split-video and scroll-gallery system, but its fashion/archive shopping model introduces a custom cursor, scroll-jacking, product prices, and complex galleries. Those patterns compete with restaurant conversion, increase mobile cost, and bury practical information.

## 9. Page architecture — guided restaurant tour

### 1. Arrival — “Fuori, Ostia di sera”

Full viewport on desktop and a carefully sized `min-height: 100dvh` hero on mobile. The storefront video establishes the address, neon sign, evening street, and open entrance. Include compact navigation, restaurant category/location context, primary reservation CTA, secondary call action, and an invitation to enter.

### 2. Threshold — “Entra”

The visual transition crosses from exterior darkness into the warm doorway. Use one wide interior/entrance photograph or a brief second clip, a short line of copy, and subtle forward motion. This chapter should feel like passing through the door, not reading an About section.

### 3. Dining room — “Dentro Golden Italy”

An immersive room view reveals light, tables, materials, and human atmosphere. Use a wide frame followed by one or two close details such as place settings, glassware, the open kitchen, hands serving, or guests in context. Do not use a generic card grid.

### 4. Table chapter — “La serata prende posto”

A more intimate sequence moves from the room to the table. Use editorial split layouts or overlapping frames with restrained scroll reveals. Copy remains sensory and short; motion must never hijack scrolling.

### 5. Dish chapter — “Dalla cucina alla tavola”

Show four or five owner-approved food photographs based on the Tripadvisor subject shortlist. This is not a menu: no category navigation, comprehensive item list, prices, cart, filters, ordering controls, or allergen claims. Each photograph may use only a confirmed dish name and an optional one-line sensory caption approved by the owner.

Recommended rhythm: one large hero dish, two alternating portrait details, one wide shared-table moment, and one final dish that visually leads toward the reservation CTA. On mobile, use a vertical editorial stack rather than a horizontal-only carousel.

### 6. Practical information — “Quando e dove”

Address, confirmed hours, phone, accessibility features, directions, and reservation method. This section must be visible and indexable without interaction.

### 7. Final reservation CTA — “Il prossimo passo è il tuo tavolo”

A short closing line, confirmed service information, and the same primary CTA used in the hero.

### Mobile fixed action

After the hero, show a safe-area-aware bottom bar with “Prenota” as primary and a phone icon plus visible “Chiama” label as secondary. Both targets must be at least 44×44px with 8px spacing.

## 10. Functional requirements

- Semantic one-page navigation with accessible anchor targets.
- Reservation link or lightweight booking flow; the provider is still unknown.
- `tel:+39065624002` once the owner confirms the number.
- Directions link to the confirmed Google Maps place.
- No full menu, menu application, prices, ordering flow, menu PDF, or catalogue navigation in this project.
- A semantic dish showcase with four or five owner-approved photographs, confirmed names, and useful Italian alt text.
- Italian first; language switch appears only if a complete English version exists.
- Restaurant structured data after address, phone, cuisine, and hours are verified; omit menu-related fields until the separate menu application exists.
- Consent-aware analytics for CTA clicks, directions, tour progress, dish-chapter views, and calls.
- Privacy/cookie controls appropriate to the final analytics and booking integrations.

## 11. Accessibility and performance guardrails

- Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus styles, logical heading order, and a skip link.
- All meaningful images need useful Italian alt text; decorative media uses empty alt text.
- Caption or describe any video content needed to understand the page.
- Ensure the primary CTA is reachable without hover, custom cursor, or motion.
- Keep micro-interactions between 150–300ms and animate only transform/opacity.
- Animate at most one or two key elements per viewport.
- Do not disable browser zoom or hijack scrolling.
- Test at 375, 768, 1024, and 1440px plus mobile landscape.
- Target no horizontal overflow and CLS below 0.1.
- Reserve media dimensions, lazy-load below-fold content, and use responsive image formats.
- The hero must remain usable when video fails, autoplay is blocked, JavaScript is unavailable, or the user requests reduced motion.

## 12. Content and trust strategy

The observed Tripadvisor score is mixed, so the landing page should not inflate or selectively present it as a high-rating badge. Trust should come from accurate information, current owned photography, an honest view of the space and dishes, easy contact, and genuine owner-approved testimonials.

If reviews are used:

- Obtain permission or use a platform-compliant embed.
- Attribute the reviewer and platform accurately.
- Never edit a quote in a way that changes its meaning.
- Show the retrieval date for dynamic ratings.
- Avoid fabricated counts, rankings, awards, or “best in Ostia” claims.
